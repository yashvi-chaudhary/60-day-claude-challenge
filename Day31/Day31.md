🚨 Day 31/60 — AI Supply Chain Control Tower

Today I stepped into a role I've never actually held: Head of Operations at a global supply chain company.

Built a full operations simulation game — single HTML file, no frameworks, no backend, runs completely offline.

How it works:
 Live disruptions hit your network in real time — port congestion, supplier delays, truck breakdowns, factory failures, demand spikes, and more. Every alert comes with a priority level and a countdown. 

You choose how to respond: expedite the shipment, switch to a backup supplier, reroute trucks, approve air freight... or ignore it and hope it goes away (it won't).
What's actually being simulated:
 7 live KPIs — Service Level, Customer Satisfaction, Inventory Health, Transportation Efficiency, Operating Cost, Revenue Protected, and Score — all shifting in real time based on your decisions. Some bad calls hit you immediately. Others (like delaying a decision) come back to bite you a few seconds later — because in real operations, consequences aren't always instant.

As the 3-minute shift runs down, disruptions come faster and multiple alerts stay active at once — just like an actual bad day in ops.
At the end: a full debrief with your final grade (A+ to D), KPI breakdown, and an operational performance summary.

Tech stack: HTML + CSS + Vanilla JS. One file. Zero dependencies.
The more of these I build, the more I realize: good operations isn't about avoiding every fire — it's about triaging fast and choosing the right lever under pressure.
