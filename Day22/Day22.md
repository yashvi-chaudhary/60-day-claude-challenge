Today I turned Claude into my Startup Advisor, VC Analyst, and Market Research team — all in one chat.

The task: validate my Final Year Project idea — an AI-Powered Student Performance Prediction Platform — the same way a VC firm would before writing a check.

I asked Claude to build a complete Startup Validation Report covering:

✅ Problem Validation (backed by real research, not assumptions)
✅ Founder-Market Fit Score
✅ TAM / SAM / SOM sizing for the Indian EdTech market
✅ Competitor Analysis (global + India-specific)
✅ ICP + Buyer Personas
✅ Risk Assessment
✅ Go / No-Go Recommendation
✅ A 30-Day Action Plan

18 pages later, here's the honest part: my founder-market fit scored 6.5/10, and the verdict was a "Conditional GO" — not a full green light.

Why? Because I have zero institutional interviews and zero pilot users so far. Every insight is grounded in real data (UDISE+, IBEF, global case studies like Georgia State University's 7% graduation-rate improvement) — but none of it means anything until a real school actually says yes.

The biggest lesson from today: an AI-generated market report can prove a problem exists. It can't prove anyone will pay you to solve it. That part is still on me.

Next up: Week 1 of my own 30-day plan — talking to real principals and teachers before writing another line of code.
