Yesterday Claude validated my startup idea like a VC firm.
Today I asked it to act as a Product Manager instead — and turn that validation into an actual build plan.

The task: take my Startup Validation Report and generate a Customer & MVP Blueprint — the kind a PM would hand an engineering team on day one.

Claude delivered, in a concise 4-page PDF:

✅ Ideal Customer Profile + Buyer Persona
✅ Top 10 Customer Pain Points
✅ Customer Journey (Awareness → Retention)
✅ Key Objections & Buying Triggers
✅ MVP Recommendation — what to build first, what NOT to build
✅ MoSCoW Prioritization
✅ Pricing Hypothesis
✅ Top 5 Risks
✅ 30-Day MVP Plan + Founder Action Sheet

The scores made me pause: Customer Clarity 58/100, but MVP Readiness only 35/100 — the lowest of all four.

Translation: I know who I'm building for. I don't yet have proof I should build it the way I've imagined. So the MVP got cut down hard — one prediction, one dashboard, one pilot. Everything else (parent app, integrations, vernacular support) got moved to "Won't Have" on purpose.

Final verdict: 🟡 Promising but Unvalidated.

Same honest thread as yesterday: the idea is fine. The proof isn't there yet. That's this week's job.
